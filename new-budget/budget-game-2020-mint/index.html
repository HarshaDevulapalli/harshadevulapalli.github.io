Notes
1.The fiscal deficit has been pegged at 3% of GDP based on the Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management (FRBM) Act, 2003. The act, which seeks to bring transparency and accountability to fiscal policy, mandates that the government limit the fiscal deficit to 3% of GDP by March 2021. These calculations do not account for off-budget items and hence understate the true extent of the budget deficit.
2. The financial allocations for each item in the budget are based on the projected estimates for 2020-21 (assuming a 10% growth rate over 2019-20 allocation - the same average annual increase in total government spending over the last decade).
3. The FCI calculation is based on the carry over liability for the last five fiscal years.
4. The allocation for judiciary reflects allocation for judicial infrastructure as well as allocation for central schemes relating to legal reforms.
5. The allocation for modernization of police relates only to the funds alloted for modernization of police force, and not for other heads of expendture related to policing.
6. The budgetary allocation shown here for railways denotes the net budgetary support, and does not include other forms of financing for railways such as through the special fund for railway safety, Rashtriya Rail Sanraksha Kosh.
